EXOTIC FRUITS FOR SPECIFIED AREAS

Many letters have been received from people requesting a 'list' of exotic fruits to grow in their particular area. In presenting the recommendations, I would like members to realise that the fruit listed, may be commercial to some extent (sometimes a very small extent) in other parts of the world, and even though they may grow well in your area, they may or may not command good prices in the local market place.

Minimum winter temperatures in the areas concerned, and the 'cold-hardiness' of the plants are the main criteria used in compiling the lists. Good tree management may help to extend the boundaries slightly into colder areas. For example, trees growing in a marginal areas, should never be allowed to go into winter with a new vegetative flush. Frost and cold wind protection also helps the trees survive under difficult climatic conditions.

It should be remembered that trees may survive or even grow well in marginal areas, but fruit production may be considerably reduced.
