WHERE DO OUR TROPICAL FRUITS COME FROM?

Before you begin to favour one region of the world over another as to which has produced the finest tropical fruits, think where do they come from? Be careful because tamarinds didn't originate in India, nor Normanton.

Key 
x = already grown commercially in Australia 
y = grown commercially in regions other than origin.  
z = grown in large numbers in specific areas in regions other than origin.  
+ = fruits of outstanding qualities worthy of expanded commercialisation in areas other than regions of origin. 

Dr Robert Knight Jr., Horticulturist with the U.S.D.A. in a paper entitled 'Origin and World Importance of Tropical and Subtropical Fruit Crops', listed 21 sites of origin of fruit crops. Of these 21 fruit crops, 9 were of American origin, 7 of Asiatic origin, 2 of Mid-East origin, 1 of African, 1 of Australian, and 1 a hybrid developed in Puerto Rico of Asiatic parents.
