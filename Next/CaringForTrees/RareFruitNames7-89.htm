RARE FRUIT NAMES

The first question asked by most people, when they are introduced to a fruit or vegetable they have never seen before is, "What is it called?" The answer is usually a common name of the plant and this is where the problem starts.

Each plant has one botanical name. This name is often too long or too difficult to remember to be used by most people, so common names are more often used.

There are no limits to the number of common names that a plant may have. Efforts are regularly made to try and reduce the number of common names used for each plant and occasionally institutions draw up lists of preferred common names or standard common names.

In 1984 B. Watson, W. Lewis, D. Maggs and P. Page compiled an 80-page booklet called AUSTROFRUIT I - checklist of tropical and sub-tropical crops' botanical and common names. This book is very comprehensive and valuable but not readily available.

The folowing list indicates preferred common names of many fruits (the first name in the common name list). To avoid confusion, the plant's botanical name is also listed.
